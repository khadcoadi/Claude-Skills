# AV Lead Email Guidelines

## Scope

These guidelines apply ONLY to AV leads. Do not draft emails for Padzilla or Unreal Bowling leads.

---

## The Formula: 3 Sentences, Under 50 Words

Every first-touch AV email follows this structure:

**Sentence 1 — Acknowledge their inquiry.**
Mirror their exact words back. If they said "2 locations," you say "2 locations." If they said "want update video," you say "updating the video." If they said "I need a free consultation," you say "your request for a consultation." Don't rephrase their words into sales language.

**Sentence 2 — Why Crunchy Tech is different.**
This is NOT about what Crunchy Tech is. It's about the OUTCOME the owner cares about. Frame around revenue, guest experience, and staying power — not equipment features.

Sentence 2 options by venue type:

- **Sports bars:** "We design AV for bars and restaurants that actually drives covers and keeps people longer — not just TVs on a wall."
- **Sports bars (alt):** "We build commercial AV for sports bars designed around one thing — filling more seats and selling more drinks."
- **Restaurants:** "We design AV for restaurants that keeps guests in their seats longer and sets the right energy room by room."
- **Restaurants (alt):** "We engineer sound and AV for restaurants around one thing — the atmosphere that makes people order another round instead of asking for the check."
- **Entertainment venues:** "We build AV for entertainment venues that turns the experience into the reason people come back."
- **General / unclear venue type:** "We build AV for bars and restaurants that's designed around guest experience and revenue — not just putting screens up."
- **Multi-location:** "We design AV systems for [bars/restaurants] that [revenue framing] — and scale across multiple locations."

Pick the version that best matches the venue type from enrichment. Adapt naturally — these are templates to riff on, not copy verbatim.

**Sentence 3 — Hand them control.**
Ask when they're free. Don't propose a specific day or time. Don't say "Would you be available for a 15-minute consultation call at your earliest convenience?" Just say it like a human.

Options:
- "Would love to hear what you're thinking. Free for a quick call this week?"
- "Happy to learn what you've got in mind. When works for a quick call?"
- "When's a good time to chat about what you're looking for?"

---

## Subject Line

Keep it short. Lowercase is fine. Make it feel like a person wrote it, not a CRM automation.

Good:
- "your AV inquiry"
- "Crafty Rooster AV"
- "following up"
- "Old Broadway video"
- "good to see you back" (for returning leads)
- "{Company Name} AV"

Bad:
- "RE: Your Recent Inquiry About Audio Visual Services"
- "Crunchy Tech — Commercial AV Solutions for Your Venue"
- "Let's Transform Your Sports Bar Experience"
- Anything with "solutions," "transform," "elevate," or "comprehensive"

## Signature

```

Crunchy Tech
(407) 545-1788
```

Use the rep's first name from the Owner field. Not full name. Not title. Not "Best regards." Not "Looking forward to hearing from you."

For returning leads where history exists, the sign-off can be slightly warmer but still brief.

## Tone Rules

### NEVER say:
- "Thanks for reaching out" (as the opening line — it's filler. Can be used mid-email if natural)
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out because..."
- "We specialize in..."
- "Our comprehensive solutions..."
- "Cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" / "best-in-class"
- "I'd love to schedule a call" (too formal — just ask "free for a call?")
- "At your earliest convenience"
- "Please don't hesitate to..."
- "Leverage" / "optimize" / "streamline"
- "Solutions" (ever)

### ALWAYS:
- Use their first name, never "Hi there" or "Dear {name}"
- Use a dash after their name: "Hey Sean —" or "Warren —"
- Mirror their exact words from the form submission
- Keep the TOTAL body (not counting signature) under 50 words
- End with a question, not a statement
- Sound like someone who texts, not someone who writes memos

### For returning leads (prior CRM history found):
- Acknowledge the history simply: "Good to see you back" / "Saw your name come through again"
- Don't retell the whole deal story — the rep will know the context from the enrichment note
- Reference that things have changed: "We've got some new stuff since we last talked"
- Tone is slightly warmer but still brief

### For leads where the Company field is garbage:
- Don't reference the company name in the email
- Lead with the product interest instead: "Got your note about AV for your bar"
- If there's no usable info at all, skip the draft and note "Insufficient context for email draft — manual outreach recommended"
